# Results log

## Scalar and chiral spinor phase

The scalar and chiral spinor simulations established numerical infrastructure:

- split-step Fourier solvers;
- vortex/defect detection;
- trajectory tracking;
- global overlap phase measurement;
- Pseudospin and Skyrmion-like texture diagnostics.

Main conclusions:

- stable defects exist;
- full clean braiding was not achieved;
- global phase often arises from extended internal spin texture rather than from local vortex cores;
- signed Skyrmion-like charge was not stable without boundary/topological constraints.

## Q-stability campaign

A compact Q(t) validation over multiple resolutions showed:

- defect count remained stable in selected chiral spinor runs;
- signed Q was not quantized;
- absolute topological density grew reproducibly.

## CP¹ fixed-boundary prototype

The CP¹/S² model with fixed north-pole boundary produced robust topological charge:

Q: -0.998794 -> -0.998791

## Multi-sector test

The multi-sector test showed stable sectors:

Q ≈ ±1, ±2, ±3

with only very small drift.
